# Blood Donation Guidelines

## Minimum Age
A donor must generally be between 18 and 65 years old.

## Minimum Weight
A donor should weigh at least 50 kg.

## Hemoglobin
The donor should have an acceptable hemoglobin level before donating blood.

## Donation Interval
Whole blood donation should usually have a minimum interval of 3 months between donations for men and 4 months for women, depending on local regulations.

## Tattoos and Piercings
Individuals who have recently received a tattoo or piercing may need to wait before donating blood. The waiting period depends on national blood donation guidelines.

## Illness
A person with fever, infection, or other temporary illness should wait until fully recovered before donating blood.

## Pregnancy
Pregnant women should not donate blood. Blood donation can usually resume after recovery following pregnancy according to medical guidance.

## Blood Pressure
People with controlled blood pressure may be eligible to donate blood depending on medical assessment.

## Diabetes
People with well-controlled diabetes may be eligible to donate blood depending on their overall health and medication.

## General Health
Donors should feel healthy and well on the day of donation.
